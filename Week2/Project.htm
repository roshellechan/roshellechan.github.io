Discover the Galapagos Islands Wildlife Tour

The Galapagos Islands are a chain of islands in the eastern Pacific Ocean. The Galapagos lie about 966 kilometers off of the Ecuadorian coast. There are thirteen major islands and a several smaller islands that make up the archipelago. Repeated volcanic eruptions helped to form the rugged mountain landscape of the Galapagos Islands.

Inspired by the Chimu Adventures blog, this map is a virtual tour of the Galapagos Islands, one of the world's most unique destinations known for their astonishing sights and captivating wildlife. Meet and learn about the friendly inhabitants of the Galapagos by clicking on each turtle icon that represents the locations of where these species can be found. Endemic species include the giant Galapagos tortoise, the marine iguana, and the Galapagos penguin.

Use this handy tool to help you plan your future visit to this wildlife-viewing paradise!
